<p><p>The brain neurointerventional device market comprises three main types: ischemia, hemorrhagic, and pathway markets. Ischemia devices target acute ischemic stroke by restoring blood flow to the brain, primarily through thrombectomy tools. Hemorrhagic devices focus on managing conditions like aneurysms and arteriovenous malformations, employing techniques such as embolization. The pathway market encompasses technologies that enhance diagnostic and therapeutic procedures, improving access to brain regions. Collectively, these markets aim to advance treatment options for various cerebrovascular conditions.</p></p>

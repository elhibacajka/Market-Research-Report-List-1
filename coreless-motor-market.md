<p><p>The coreless motor market is primarily divided into two types: brushless motors and brushed motors. Brushless motors, characterized by their lack of brushes, offer higher efficiency, reliability, and longer lifespan due to reduced wear and tear. In contrast, brushed motors use physical brushes for commutation, resulting in simpler design and lower initial cost but shorter lifespan and more maintenance. Both types serve various applications across industries, from robotics to automotive, driven by the need for efficient and compact motor solutions.</p></p>

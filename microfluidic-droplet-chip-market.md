<p><ul><li>Passive</li><li>Active</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The microfluidic droplet chip market is segmented into passive and active types. Passive droplet chips rely on surface tension and capillary forces to manipulate fluids without external intervention, making them simpler and cost-effective for applications like biochemical assays. In contrast, active droplet chips employ external mechanisms, such as micropumps or electric fields, to control droplet movement and processing, enabling more complex operations and higher precision. This distinction underlines the versatility and diverse applications of microfluidic technology in research and diagnostics.</p></p>

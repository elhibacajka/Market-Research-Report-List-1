<p><p>The presbyopia correction device market comprises various types designed to address age-related vision changes. Corneal inlays are small devices inserted into the cornea to enhance near vision while maintaining distance vision, often utilized in refractive surgery. Scleral implants, on the other hand, are positioned in the sclera to improve vision by altering the eye's optical properties. Other devices may include specialized contact lenses and surgical techniques that aim to restore near vision, offering options for individuals experiencing presbyopia.</p></p>

<p><ul><li>Anionic</li><li>Cationic</li><li>Bisexual</li><li>Non-ionic</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Silicone surface additives are categorized based on their ionic characteristics, influencing their compatibility and application. Anionic additives are negatively charged, enhancing wetting and dispersing properties in coatings. Cationic additives carry a positive charge, improving adhesion and promoting antimicrobial properties. Bisexual additives possess both anionic and cationic features, offering versatile performance in various formulations. Non-ionic additives, lacking any charge, provide stability and compatibility across diverse systems, making them suitable for various applications in paints, inks, and coatings.</p></p>
